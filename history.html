History of Indian Independence

The Indian independence movement was a monumental struggle against British colonial rule that lasted over a century. From the early revolts in 1857 — known as the First War of Independence — to the eventual freedom in 1947, the movement saw participation from leaders, revolutionaries, and ordinary citizens alike.

The 20th century saw the rise of mass movements led by Mahatma Gandhi, advocating non-violent resistance or "Satyagraha". He led major campaigns such as the Non-Cooperation Movement (1920), the Salt March (1930), and the Quit India Movement (1942). Other leaders like Subhas Chandra Bose formed the Indian National Army (INA) to fight British forces militarily.

Revolutionaries such as Bhagat Singh, Rajguru, and Sukhdev inspired youth with their fearless actions. Thousands were imprisoned or martyred in their pursuit of a free India. The unity of Hindus, Muslims, Sikhs, and other communities under one cause was a testament to India's diversity and spirit.

Finally, after years of sacrifices and global pressure, India gained independence on August 15, 1947 — marking the end of colonial rule and the birth of the world’s largest democracy.
